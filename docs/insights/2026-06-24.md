**Proposed new marts / metrics**

1. **Stop‑Level Lateness Index (SLLI)**  
   *Definition*: `SLLI = (median_delay_minutes) × (1 – OTP%)`.  
   *Purpose*: Highlights stops where even modest median delays combine with very low OTP (e.g., 136 Georgetowne Pl: 12.7 × 1 = 12.7). Rank‑ordered SLLI can feed dashboards for targeted interventions (traffic signal priority, stop‑spacing changes).

2. **Route‑Corridor Cluster KPI**  
   *Definition*: For each identified corridor (e.g., Georgetown/Boundary‑Road, North Shore‑Shore Rd, Franklin‑Shuttle), compute:  
   - weighted‑average median delay (delay × stop volume)  
   - proportion of stops with OTP < 20 %  
   - “Late‑Stop Concentration” = (# stops median > 5 min) / (total stops in corridor).  
   *Purpose*: Supplies a single view of corridor health for operations and capital planning (e.g., dedicated bus lanes).

3. **Early/Late Balance Score (ELBS) by Mode**  
   *Definition*: `ELBS = (Sum of early‑stop minutes) / (Sum of late‑stop minutes)`. Compute separately for **bus**, **shuttle**, and **rail‑linked bus** modes.  
   *Purpose*: Quantifies whether a mode’s schedule bias is early or late; a low ELBS (<0.5) flags a mode that needs schedule padding or real‑time control (e.g., for routes 24, 350, 504). The score can trigger automated alerts when the trend shifts beyond a configurable threshold.
